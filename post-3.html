Culture

 

Posted by Katrina Lucas on December 26th 2020

I was born and raised in Barbados which is in the eastern Caribbean.  Growing up as a child in Barbados my view of culture was a way of life. It was viewed as how I interact with those around me, the way in which history was passed down through folk songs, from the way I spend me leisure time on our beautiful beaches or roaming our tropical landscape. 

My culture, my Barbadian culture means, being friendly
to persons on the street, chatting a making small talk while you wait on the
bus to arrive or having a relationship with your neigbours to the extent that
they are part of your extended family, being helpful to those in your
community, this is my Culture, and my culture is who I am. 

Swann et al (2004) define culture as ‘a set of everyday practices and associated beliefs, ideas and values that characterize a particular community or group’.  How I interact with those around its testament to my culture. As an individual I am constantly developing and changing and so is my culture. Culture is not
stagnant, it is dynamic always changing and evolving (Nathan,2010).

Culture is more than an experience or feeling; it is also what develops my identity. Identity can define as ‘how a person understands his/her relationship to the world, how that relationship is structured across time and space, and how the person understands possibilities for the future’ (Norton, 2013:45). I cannot separate my culture from my identity, it is who I am.

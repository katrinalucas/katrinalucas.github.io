Ethnography

 

Posted by Katrina Lucas on December 19th 2020

Ethnography can be defined according to Roberts et al
(2011) as the ‘study of a group’s social and cultural practices from an insider’s perspective’.

Ethnography research allows the researcher to conduct qualitative research through the lens of an observer of a real-life experience or environment to gain a deeper understanding of a situation or problem.

However, in contrast, an autoethnography place me as
the focus of my research to reflect on my own experiences in relation to a particular aspect of my life in connection to my cultural experiences.

Moreover, an autoethnography approaches research and writing that seek to bring clarity and systematically analyze personal experience to understand cultural interactions.
